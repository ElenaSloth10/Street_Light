Lantern

In one cozy city, on one of the narrow winding streets, there lived a lantern. He faithfully served his city, lighting the road every evening. In the morning he saw fussy people, and in the evening illuminated the path for loving couples. The street lamp really liked his work. He always greeted his neighbors, fellow lanterns. Colleagues stood too far away and there was no way to talk to them.

The lantern liked to peer into the sky and every evening he was saying goodbye to the sun and then meeting the moon and stars. It seemed like they were one big family having fun together. The lantern really enjoyed watching them and in cloudy weather he missed them very much. On such days, he secretly watched the windows of the neighboring house. Someone lived behind each window, but what was most touching were the children, who were constantly telling each other something and being rowdy. They were the ones he envied the most. He was lonely and only occasionally had the opportunity to talk with the cheerful birds that here and there sat on a lantern.

One day, schoolchildren planted a linden sapling near him. The street lamp was very curious who appeared nearby. And he asked:

- Who are you?

- I am a linden tree, - the foliage whispered.

From that time on, the street lamp and the linden tree became best friends.

The lantern constantly told the leaves something, and quite quickly the seedling grew and turned into a spreading tree and began to reach the top of its friend. The leaves were constantly whispering, telling funny stories, and the lantern loved them very much. But when autumn came and the leaves fell, it made him very sad. He was terribly sad all winter, felt lonely again and was really looking forward to spring.

Then the linden tree decided to help its friend. She directed her branches towards him and soon the lantern was in her arms. So that the lantern would not be so sad.

And one day a mischievous neighbor settled among the branches - a little squirrel. She made herself a hollow and began to live in it. The street lamp was very happy with its new resident. And soon they became friends. A year later, the squirrel had two little children. For the first time, the lantern felt that they had a real big family. This is how they still live.
